# Reflection

During this assignment, one of the main challenges was balancing the different needs and expectations of the various stakeholders involved in the system. Each stakeholder group had its own priorities and concerns, which sometimes conflicted with one another. For instance, students with disabilities required a system that is highly accessible, easy to use, and capable of adapting to their individual learning needs. At the same time, IT staff were more focused on system performance, security, and scalability, ensuring that the platform could handle multiple users without failure. Lecturers were primarily concerned with tracking student progress and improving engagement. Balancing these different perspectives required careful consideration, as improving one aspect of the system could negatively affect another. For example, adding advanced AI features could enhance personalization but also increase system complexity and resource requirements.

Another challenge was defining requirements that were clear, specific, and measurable. It was important to avoid vague statements and instead develop requirements that could be tested and evaluated. This required thinking carefully about how each requirement would be implemented in practice. For example, rather than stating that the system should be “fast,” it was necessary to define measurable performance indicators such as page load times and response speeds. Similarly, accessibility requirements needed to be clearly defined to ensure that the system meets the needs of students with disabilities.

Distinguishing between functional and non-functional requirements also required a deeper understanding of system design. Functional requirements describe what the system should do, such as providing personalized learning content, while non-functional requirements describe how the system should perform, such as being secure, reliable, and scalable. Ensuring that both types of requirements were properly addressed helped to create a more complete and realistic system design.

In addition, applying an Agile approach highlighted the importance of flexibility in requirements engineering. As the system evolves, requirements may change based on new insights or stakeholder feedback. This reinforced the idea that requirements are not fixed but should be continuously refined throughout the development process.

Overall, this assignment improved my understanding of requirements engineering and emphasized the importance of designing systems that are both user-centered and technically feasible. It also highlighted the need for clear communication and careful planning when developing complex systems.
