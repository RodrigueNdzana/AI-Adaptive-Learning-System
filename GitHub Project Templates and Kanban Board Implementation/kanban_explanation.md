# Kanban Board Explanation

## What is a Kanban Board?

A Kanban board is a visual project management tool used to track tasks as they move through different stages of a workflow. It helps teams understand progress, identify bottlenecks, and manage work efficiently.

---

## How Our Kanban Board Works

Our Kanban board includes the following columns:

- To Do – Tasks that are planned but not started
- In Progress – Tasks currently being worked on
- Testing – Tasks undergoing quality assurance
- Blocked – Tasks that cannot proceed due to issues
- Done – Completed tasks

Tasks move from left to right as progress is made.

---

## Work-in-Progress (WIP) Limits

We limit work-in-progress to **3 tasks per column** to:

- Prevent overload
- Maintain focus
- Improve productivity
- Reduce bottlenecks

---

## Support for Agile Principles

The Kanban board supports Agile by:

- Enabling **continuous delivery**
- Providing **real-time visibility**
- Supporting **flexibility and adaptation**
- Encouraging **incremental progress**

This ensures efficient development of the AI Adaptive Learning System.
